## Research Overview

My research sits at the intersection of biomaterials, 
3D bioprinting, and tissue engineering, with a strong 
focus on developing innovative solutions for regenerative 
medicine. From engineering cardiac patches to investigating 
HIV's impact on stem cells, my work spans multiple 
cutting-edge fields united by a common goal: engineering 
functional tissues that restore and repair the human body.

---

## Current Research — Cardiac Tissue Engineering
*Medical College of Wisconsin | 2025 - Present*

My current postdoctoral work focuses on cardiac tissue 
engineering, developing patches and grafts using both 
established and novel polymer systems. Key areas include:

- **Novel Polymer Development** — Designing and testing 
  new polymers for cardiac patch fabrication with improved 
  mechanical and biological properties
- **Hydrogel Formation** — Engineering advanced hydrogels 
  using recombinant DNA-based strategies and cutting-edge 
  DNA aptamer technology for cardiac repair
- **3D Bioprinting** — Fabricating complex cardiac 
  constructs using advanced bioprinting techniques
- **Bioreactor Systems** — Enhancing tissue maturation 
  and mechanical conditioning of engineered cardiac 
  constructs

---

## Postdoctoral Research — Bioprinting & Virology
*First Postdoctoral Fellowship | 2022 - 2024*

I combined 3D bioprinting with immunology by engineering 
bone marrow models to investigate the impact of HIV on 
hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs). Key contributions:

- **3D Bioprinted Bone Marrow Models** — Pioneered novel 
  platforms recreating the hematopoietic stem cell niche
- **HIV & HSC Research** — Investigated HIV's impact on 
  stem cells within a physiologically relevant 3D 
  environment resulting in a **granted patent**
- **Neurovascular Research** — Explored crosstalk 
  disruption at the neurovascular unit during HIV 
  infection of the CNS
- **Neuronal Bioprinting** — Developed 3D printed neuronal 
  networks contributing to neural tissue engineering
- **Recognition** — Travel Award at SNIP Conference, 
  Internal Research Grant ($4,000)

## PhD Research — Hydrogels & Biomaterial Innovation
*Rowan University | 2018 - 2022*

My doctoral research focused on developing novel 
biomaterial strategies for tissue engineering:

- **Osteochondral Interface Engineering** — Designing 
  biomaterials for cartilage and bone repair
- **Click Chemistry Hydrogels** — Developing hydrogels 
  using bioorthogonal click chemistry for precise 
  material control
- **Antimicrobial Peptides** — Screening and designing 
  peptide-based biomaterials to prevent biofilm formation
- **Bioactive PCL** — Creating facile methods for 
  functionalizing polycaprolactone with bioactive signals
- **Publications** — 7 peer-reviewed papers including 
  2 first-author publications
- **Conferences** — Presentations at BMES, SFB, and ORS

## Master's Research — Orthopedic Bioprinting
*Rowan University | 2016 - 2018*

My Master's research focused on 3D printing orthopedic 
implants to prevent post-surgical infections:

- **Antibiotic-Infused Implants** — Designed and tested 
  3D printed femoral implants eluting antibiotics to 
  prevent infections following total knee arthroplasty
- **Publications** — 2 peer-reviewed papers including 
  1 first-author publication
