Hi, I'm Mehdi!

I am a biomedical engineer and postdoctoral researcher at the 
Medical College of Wisconsin, specializing in biomaterials, 
3D bioprinting, and tissue engineering. My research focuses 
on developing innovative hydrogel-based solutions and 
bioprinted constructs for regenerative medicine, with a 
current emphasis on cardiac tissue engineering using novel 
polymer systems, recombinant DNA-based hydrogels, and 
DNA aptamer technology.

Previously, I completed my PhD at Rowan University, where 
I specialized in hydrogel design using click chemistry and 
peptide-based biomaterials for osteochondral tissue 
engineering. During my first postdoctoral fellowship, I 
pioneered 3D bioprinted bone marrow models to investigate 
HIV's impact on hematopoietic stem cells — work that 
resulted in a granted patent.

I am passionate about translational research and committed 
to engineering the next generation of biomaterial solutions 
for regenerative medicine.
