## About Me

I am a biomedical engineer with extensive expertise in 
biomaterials, 3D bioprinting, and tissue engineering. 
My research journey spans multiple cutting-edge fields, 
from hydrogel development to regenerative medicine, 
with a strong focus on translational applications.

## Research Background

My academic journey began with a Master's degree focused 
on 3D printing with plastic materials, where I developed 
a strong foundation in additive manufacturing techniques. 
I then pursued a PhD in tissue engineering, specializing 
in hydrogel formation using click chemistry and peptide-based 
biomaterials — work that laid the groundwork for my later 
research in advanced biomaterial design.

During my first postdoctoral fellowship, I combined 3D 
bioprinting with immunology by engineering bone marrow 
models to investigate the impact of HIV on hematopoietic 
stem cells (HSCs). This interdisciplinary work bridged 
biomaterial science with virology, providing novel insights 
into how HIV affects the stem cell niche. This led to the filing of a patent application for the novel idea. I further expanded my bioprinting expertise by developing 3D printed neuronal 
networks, contributing to the field of neural tissue engineering.

## Current Research

In my current postdoctoral position, I am focused on 
cardiac tissue engineering, developing patches and grafts 
using both established and novel polymer systems. My work 
integrates advanced hydrogel formation strategies — including 
recombinant DNA-based hydrogels and cutting-edge DNA aptamer 
technology — to engineer functional cardiac constructs. 
I also work extensively with bioreactor systems to enhance 
tissue maturation and mechanical conditioning of engineered 
constructs.
