## About Me

I am a biomedical engineer with extensive expertise in 
biomaterials, 3D bioprinting, and tissue engineering. 
My research journey spans multiple cutting-edge fields, 
from hydrogel development to regenerative medicine, with 
a strong focus on translational applications.

---

## Research Background

**Master's Research — Orthopedic Bioprinting**
My academic journey began with a Master's degree focused 
on 3D printing orthopedic implants, specifically targeting 
post-infection prevention in total knee arthroplasty. 
This work resulted in two publications, including one 
first-author paper, and established my foundation in 
additive manufacturing and orthopedic biomaterials.

**PhD Research — Hydrogels & Biomaterial Innovation**
I pursued my PhD in tissue engineering with a focus on 
osteochondral interface engineering, antimicrobial peptide 
development, and creating facile fabrication methods for 
bioactive PCL constructs. My doctoral work specialized in 
hydrogel formation using click chemistry and peptide-based 
biomaterials, producing 7 publications including two 
first-author papers. During this period, I actively 
contributed to the scientific community through numerous 
poster presentations at leading conferences including 
BMES, SFB, and ORS, as well as invited talks at 
national conferences.

**First Postdoctoral Fellowship — Bioprinting & Virology**
During my first postdoctoral fellowship, I combined 3D 
bioprinting with immunology by engineering bone marrow 
models to investigate the impact of HIV on hematopoietic 
stem cells (HSCs). This interdisciplinary work bridged 
biomaterial science with virology, providing novel insights 
into how HIV affects the stem cell niche, and resulted in 
a filed patent application for this novel platform. I 
further expanded my bioprinting expertise by developing 
3D printed neuronal networks, contributing to the field 
of neural tissue engineering. This work resulted in two 
co-authored publications, presentations at two BMES 
Annual Meetings, including an invited talk, a talk at 
CMBE, two poster presentations at SNIP with a Travel 
Award, a granted patent, and an internal research 
grant of $4,000.

## Current Research

I recently joined my second postdoctoral position three 
months ago, where I am focused on cardiac tissue 
engineering. My current work involves developing patches 
and grafts using both established and novel polymer 
systems. This research integrates advanced hydrogel 
formation strategies — including recombinant DNA-based 
hydrogels and cutting-edge DNA aptamer technology — to 
engineer functional cardiac constructs. I also work 
extensively with bioreactor systems to enhance tissue 
maturation and mechanical conditioning of engineered 
constructs and the mechanobiology properties of the endothelial cells.
