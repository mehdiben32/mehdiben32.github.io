## Research Overview

My research sits at the intersection of biomaterials, 
3D bioprinting, and tissue engineering, with a strong 
focus on developing innovative solutions for regenerative 
medicine. From engineering cardiac patches to investigating 
HIV's impact on stem cells, my work spans multiple 
cutting-edge fields united by a common goal: engineering 
functional tissues that restore and repair the human body.

---

## Current Research — Cardiac Tissue Engineering

My current postdoctoral work at the Medical College of 
Wisconsin focuses on cardiac tissue engineering, developing 
patches and grafts using both established and novel polymer 
systems. Key areas include:

- **Novel Polymer Development** — Designing and testing 
  new polymers for cardiac patch fabrication with improved 
  mechanical and biological properties
- **Hydrogel Formation** — Engineering advanced hydrogels 
  using recombinant DNA-based strategies and cutting-edge 
  DNA aptamer technology for cardiac repair
- **3D Bioprinting** — Fabricating complex cardiac constructs 
  using advanced bioprinting techniques
- **Bioreactor Systems** — Enhancing tissue maturation and 
  mechanical conditioning of engineered cardiac constructs

---

## Hydrogels & Biomaterials

Hydrogel design and biomaterial development have been 
central themes throughout my research career. My expertise 
includes:

- **Click Chemistry** — Developing hydrogels using 
  bioorthogonal click chemistry reactions for precise 
  control of material properties
- **Peptide-Based Biomaterials** — Engineering bioactive 
  peptide sequences to enhance cell-material interactions
- **Recombinant DNA Hydrogels** — Pioneering DNA-based 
  hydrogel formation strategies for tissue engineering
- **DNA Aptamer Technology** — Applying novel aptamer 
  technology to create smart, responsive biomaterials
- **Antimicrobial Biomaterials** — Screening and designing 
  antimicrobial peptide hydrogels to prevent biofilm 
  formation

---

## 3D Bioprinting & Biofabrication

3D bioprinting has been a cornerstone of my research 
journey, spanning multiple tissue types and applications:

- **Cardiac Tissue** — Bioprinting patches and grafts 
  for cardiac repair
- **Bone Marrow Models** — Engineering 3D bioprinted 
  bone marrow niches to study HIV's impact on 
  hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) — resulting in 
  a **granted patent**
- **Neuronal Networks** — Developing 3D printed neuronal 
  constructs for neural tissue engineering
- **Orthopedic Implants** — Designing antibiotic-infused 
  3D printed implants to prevent post-surgical infections 
  in total knee arthroplasty

---

## HIV & Stem Cell Biology

During my first postdoctoral fellowship, I pioneered an 
interdisciplinary research program combining bioprinting 
with virology:

- Engineered **3D bioprinted bone marrow models** to 
  recreate the hematopoietic stem cell niche
- Investigated **HIV's impact on HSCs** within a 
  physiologically relevant 3D environment
- Explored the **neurovascular unit disruption** during 
  HIV infection of the CNS
- Developed novel platforms that bridge **biomaterial 
  science and infectious disease research**
- Work resulted in a **granted patent** for the novel 
  bone marrow bioprinting platform
